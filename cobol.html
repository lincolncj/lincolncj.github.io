Once Upon a Time...

In 1959, Grace Hopper and her team at the Department of Defense created COBOL (Common Business Oriented Language). This revolutionary programming language was designed to be readable by business people, not just programmers.

Grace Hopper, known as "Amazing Grace," was a computer programming pioneer who believed that programming languages should use English-like words instead of cryptic symbols.

👩‍💻💡

COBOL Powers Everything

Today, COBOL runs the financial world! Banks, airlines, government systems, and insurance companies all rely on COBOL for their critical operations.

Every time you withdraw money from an ATM, book a flight, or process a government form, there's a good chance COBOL is working behind the scenes to make it happen.

🏦✈️🏛️

Verbose But Clear

COBOL is famous for being verbose - meaning it uses a lot of words. Instead of cryptic abbreviations, COBOL reads like English sentences:

MOVE SALARY TO PAYCHECK.
ADD OVERTIME TO PAYCHECK.
DISPLAY "Payment processed".

This makes COBOL programs easier to read and maintain, even decades later!

📝➡️💰

Y2K Hero

When the year 2000 approached, the world panicked about the "Y2K bug" - computers might fail when dates changed from 99 to 00.

COBOL programmers became superheroes overnight! Companies desperately needed them to update millions of lines of legacy COBOL code. These "COBOL cowboys" saved the digital world from chaos.

🗓️💥🦸

Still Running Strong

Did you know that 80% of in-person transactions and 95% of ATM swipes still rely on COBOL? Despite being over 60 years old, COBOL processes an estimated 3 trillion dollars in commerce daily!

The next time someone says "old technology is useless," remember COBOL - proving that sometimes the classics never go out of style.
